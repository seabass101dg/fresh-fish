What would you say... ya do here?

I work for a government, non-profit organization specializing in security assistance and live-action role playing. Most people call it the US Army.

I initially commissioned in 2014 in the Infantry (MOS 11A) and headed off to Fort Benning for Infantry Basic Officer Leader Course (IBOLC). Soon after, went to Fort Bliss, TX and worked as a rifle platoon leader and assistant operations officer for several years before being selected for transfer to Network Systems Engineer (FA 26A) in 2017. A little bit later in 2018, found myself at Fort Gordon, GA for the Signal Captains' Career Course (SCCC) and Network Systems Engineer Course (NSEC).

I graduated NSEC in May 2019 then shipped off to Afghanistan to work as the director for the Regional Network Operations and Security Center of Afghanistan. I oversaw the operation and maintenance (O&M) of all of the LANs for US Forces in Afghanistan including user-facing Microsoft services. The assignment greatly expanded my depth of knowledge in systems and automation and solidified my experience in troubleshooting of switched environments. I also had the opportunity to manage several engineering and integration (E&I) projects.

My work gives me plenty of opportunities to learn, but I'm always playing with my home servers, various other web clients, and virtual machines and networks. I've gotten pretty handy in Bash and PowerShell scripting, Python, Java, C#, and version control using Git. I've messed with C, C++, Ruby on Rails, SQL along with HTML/CSS. By now, I've mastered Cisco IOS, Cisco UCM and VoIP, and MS Active Directory. I wouldn't consider myself a Renaissance Man quite yet, but I'm having a good time regardless.
